Stress & Recovery

As you know there is a lot of material to learn and review for the test and you might think the more you study the better you will do. We have important news for you - this is not necessarily true.

MORE ≠ BETTER

Think about the athlete who is training for the Olympics. As the date gets closer and closer, does the athlete train harder and harder? No. The athlete must pace himself and build in “down” time, both physically and mentally. For him to perform at his peak, he must be well rested and focused. This is important for you too.

THE 90 / 20 RULE

Your brain can only focus for about 90 minutes at a time. Afterwards, you need a 20 minute break to renew your energy before you refocus on studying.





THE NIGHT BEFORE THE EXAM

It’s important to come to the test refreshed and focused. Sometimes this is difficult as there are so many thoughts running through your mind.

The night before the test, take 10 minutes (on a timer) and write down every thought you have – whether related to the test or not. This will empty your head of “noise” and allow you to relax and fall asleep.

Sleep

It is very important to get enough sleep especially while studying for the test. If you are sleep deprived, you cannot focus your attention optimally nor learn efficiently. While you sleep your brain is working to consolidate memory – you are learning while you sleep! Your brain is understanding how things fit together and how to summarize the data you’ve studied.

If you are having problems falling asleep or staying asleep because your mind is too active, try these exercises:

Breathing Exercise

Guided Imagery

  • Put your tongue behind your upper front teeth
  • Breathe in through your nose to the count of 4
  • Hold your breath to the count of 7
  • Blow out your breath forcefully to the count of 8

Sometimes you can’t shut down your thoughts. Guided Imagery can help you de-clutter your busy mind and help you relax and feel comfortable.

MINDFULNESS

As the test gets closer, you may feel anxious and feel your mind is overloaded. This is normal and it’s important not to judge yourself. This Mindfulness exercise will help you refocus your mind. By paying attention to the present moment and observing your surroundings, senses, thoughts and feelings, you clear your mind and become more relaxed.
